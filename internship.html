Internship Search

What is an Internship?

Internships are pivotal experiences for UMSI students, providing real-world learning, professional connections, and a chance to apply classroom theories in professional settings. They are often a bridge between your academic career and your first full-time job.

For many Information students, an internship is where theoretical concepts like user-centered design, data pipelines, and information architecture come to life. It's a risk-free environment to test out a specific industry, company culture, or role before committing to a full-time career.

Steps to Landing Your Dream Internship

The internship search is a marathon, not a sprint. Follow these proven steps to maximize your chances of securing a great role:

  • 1. Define Your Goals: Are you looking for a UX role at a big tech company, or a data analytics position at a non-profit? Narrowing your focus helps tailor your search.
  • 2. Prepare Your Materials: Update your resume and portfolio. Ensure your LinkedIn profile is polished and up-to-date. (Visit our Resumes page for help).
  • 3. Network Aggressively: Don't just apply online. Attend UMSI career fairs, conduct informational interviews, and reach out to alumni. (See our Networking guide).
  • 4. Apply Consistently: Set a weekly goal for applications. Quality matters more than quantity, so tailor your cover letters and resumes to each specific role.
  • 5. Practice Interviewing: Utilize UMSI mock interview resources and practice articulating your project experiences using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Where to Find Internships

  • UMSI Career Resources
    Specific tools and job boards curated exclusively for Information science students by the CDO.
  • LinkedIn Jobs
    A great place to find niche roles, track company updates, and see where UMSI alumni are working.
  • Built In
    Excellent platform for discovering tech internships at startups and established tech companies across major US hubs.

Internship FAQ

  • When should I start my search?
    Many large companies (especially in tech, finance, and consulting) recruit for summer internships during the early fall semester (August - October). Start early! However, startups and smaller organizations often hire "just-in-time" in the spring semester.
  • Can I get academic credit?
    Yes, many UMSI programs allow or require internships for credit. You will typically need to submit an internship proposal and enroll in a specific course. Check with your academic advisor for specifics.
  • What if I don't have any prior tech experience?
    Highlight your academic projects, classwork, and transferable skills. Employers understand you are a student; they are looking for potential, problem-solving abilities, and a willingness to learn.
