Internship Search

What is an Internship?

Internships are pivotal experiences for UMSI students, providing real-world learning, professional connections, and a chance to apply classroom knowledge in diverse information environments.

Whether you’re interested in user experience, data analysis, library science, or information policy, landing an internship can help launch your career and clarify your professional goals. UMSI encourages students to start early and use every available resource — including personalized support, digital tools, and extensive campus networks.

While UMSI students are well-trained candidates for employers, you may still need some special techniques to help you find internships successfully!

Tips for a Successful Internship Search

  1. Start Early: Begin your search several months in advance. Many summer internships recruit during the fall semester!
  2. Reflect on Your Goals: What skills do you want to develop? What kinds of organizations interest you? Define your priorities before starting your search.
  3. Customize Your Application Materials: Always tailor your resume and cover letter for each position. Highlight class projects, leadership roles, and technical skills relevant to the internship.
  4. Tailor for Each Opportunity: Customize your resume for every job or internship you pursue.
  5. Stay Organized: Track application deadlines, requirements, and communication with employers. Use a simple spreadsheet or task manager.
  6. Follow Up: Send thank you notes after interviews. This keeps you on the employer’s radar and demonstrates professionalism.
